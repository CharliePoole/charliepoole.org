XP Haiku

In a discussion thread on the Extreme Customering list, Kent Beck happened to say that he'd like to be able to write haiku. A brief flurry of poetry from various folks ensued.

Just for fun, I captured some of them on this page. I'd love to receive more, particularly those that actually express the XP values and practices.

As you might expect from our group, some of the verse is self-referential. Since we've been giving Kent lots of advice, he decided to thank us in true haiku style, starting from an observation of nature:

Hawk meets rabbit
So writing advice comes
Email winging

Hmmm... what's he trying to tell us? Dossy Shiobara certainly isn't pleased. He overcomes his normal shyness to comment:

Kent Beck writes about
hawks and rabbits and email
but he makes no sense.

Meanwhile, Joshua Kerievsky is just warming up, as he wonders whether to go for a haiku version of his workbook:

it has been years
since I wrote a haiku
hello old friend

Dossy is apparently planning to rewrite the white book as haiku. So far, it's going well - he's completed the title page:

Extreme Programming
Embrace Change, told by Kent Beck
Write Software Better

I'd like to get more and better ones that talk about the values and practices. Here's one I wrote.

Get ready, fire, aim
Re-aim until arrival
Courage required

Jaap Beetstra had something to say about stories, tests and emergence.

Customer wishes
Programmers make all tests pass
Design emerges

Here's one that's dear to my TDDing heart, contributed by Apoorva Muralidhara

The test bar glows red
I code with simplicity
The test passes now
